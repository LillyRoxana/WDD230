Mexico City has 8 important archeological sites. The 8 archaeological zones of Mexico City are Cuicuilco, Santa Cruz Acalpixca, El Templo Mayor, Acozaco, Teotihuacan, Tenayuca, Santa Cecilia, and Tlatelolco. The most popular and most visited are El Templo Mayor in the center of the city and the Pyramids of Teotihuacan (pictured), about 50 kilometers northeast of the town.

Mexico City is one of the cities with the most museums in the world. The Mexican capital is the second city with the most museums in the world, only behind London, the capital of England. Data from the Ministry of Culture indicate that there are 168 museums in the Mexican capital, including the Museum of Memory and Tolerance, the Museum of Mexico City, the National Museum of Anthropology and History, and the National Museum of History, Chapultepec Castle.

The Chapultepec Forest has 686.05 hectares, it is the largest urban park in Latin America and receives 15 million visitors per year. It is divided into three sections. It is a historical, tourist, environmental and recreational reference for national and foreign visitors. The word Chapultepec comes from the Nahuatl "Chapulli" which means grasshopper and "Tepe" (tl) hill, "Chapulin Hill", which the Aztecs considered sacred and with supernatural powers. They were the ones who named it this way in the 13th century before founding Tenochtitlán. Chapultepec Forest is considered the lung of Mexico City for its environmental services such as air cleaning, noise reduction, groundwater recharge, refuge for migratory birds and shelter for native fauna. It is the most important urban green area in Mexico.

Mexico City's Zócalo is the second-largest square in the world and the first among Spanish-speaking countries. Also known as Plaza de la Constitución, it has an area of approximately 47,000 square meters. The Zócalo is the heart of the Historic Center and is surrounded by the Metropolitan Cathedral of Mexico City, the Old City Hall, the National Palace, hotels, and restaurants.

Mexico City has the longest subway train in Latin America. The subway has a total of 12 lines, 195 stations, and, therefore, approximately 226 kilometers long. This makes it the most extensive subway network in Latin America. In the second position is Santiago, the capital of Chile. The most extensive subway network in the world is in Seoul, South Korea.
